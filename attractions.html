This is a great place to go if you just want to look at exotic animals that you can’t see anywhere else. The park opened in 1894 and has been going strong ever since then. The place is about 15 acres of land that houses creatures of many types. Seneca Park and Zoo is opened Monday through Saturday at 10:00am to 3:00pm and these are winter hours. It has a wide variety of bird, reptiles and mammals that you can check out. Some of the most notable animals are the meerkats, the white rhino, the eastern massasauga rattlesnake, the king vulture, the snow leopards and the polar bear. As great as their selection of animals are they have a small selection of amphibians and fish, the two fish species in the zoo are local to Rochester and they also have a reef tank. The amphibians in their zoo consist mostly of frogs with the exception of the Hellbender which is a salamander. The frog species that I would consider are the poison dart frogs and the lemur leaf frogs. If you like animals and are looking to check out some out on a beautiful day then the Seneca Park and Zoo is perfect. Bringing someone along would make the experience even more enjoyable so that you both could partake in a joyous experience. Kids are more than welcome to come as the Seneca Park and Zoo has events for younger kids and they are sure to love the animals that are in the exhibits.

The Seabreeze Amusement Park is a place where crazy experiences are bound to happen. Filled with many thrill rides that are sure to fill your appetite of fun and if you are really hungry the park offers a wide variety of food from grilled food to waffles the amusement park offers just about anything you want. The park opens around the summertime so it is only limited to one season like most parks. Also, if the summer heat is too intense for you the park also has a waterpark that feature five thrill rides that is sure to cool you down and give you the time of your life. Some of the rides that are featured in the park are the Wave Swinger, Revolution 360, Sea Dragon, the Screamin’ Eagle and the Log Flume. All these rides are located in the amusement park and are filled with pure excitement. Some of the rides that are located in the waterpark are the Wave, Helix, Soak Zone, Hydro Racer and the inner tube slides, which are sure to cool you down while you also have fun. With all these fun rides nothing is more exciting and old fashioned then a rollercoaster and the Seabreeze Amusement Park offers several rollercoaster rides, such as the family friendly Bobsleds and Bear Trax, and the more intense Jack Rabbit and Whirlwind.

The MAG is a place that features art from around the world and some can be dated to be about 5,000 years old. The gallery holds 12,000 collections of art and is considered the best balanced outside of metropolitan New York City. The Gallery was founded in 1913 and was meant to be a memorial to the founders son, but now the gallery is owned by the University of Rochester and is one of the few university-affiliated art museums in the country that also serves as a community art museum. The gallery has exhibitions that are either currently on display or are upcoming and can range from just about anything about art like fashion, painting and sculptures. As you explore the permanent collection you notice that there is art from Africa, American, Ancient Americas, Ancient times, Asia, Europe, Middle East, Native American and Oceania. The art is permanent for a reason and that is because some of these pieces are rare and unique to most galleries. A lot of them are sculptures and paintings made many thousands of years ago and if you are an art enthusiast then this is surely the one place you have to visit.

Home to the minor league Rochester Red Wings the frontier field is a perfect place if you like baseball even if it is in the minor league. The field opened for baseball in 1997 and opened for a crowd of 13,227. This is closest place for professional baseball outside of the New York City area. It has been opened to the public for about 20 years now. Other events have been held in this field with the first event being the Beach Boys concert in 1996 and the first sporting event being when the Rochester Raging Bulls defeated Impact, 3-2. The Frontier Field is filled with a wide selection of dining options so you can enjoy your favorite food and drink while watching a good baseball game or some other event. Some of the food vendors include Pop ‘n’ Hots, Black Angus Grill, Say Cheese, Mama Mittsy’s, Batter up, Ice Cream Plus, ‘Sup Dog, Home Plate and much more. If you are looking to buying a ticket for a baseball game a reserved seat will cost $8.00, while a premium seat costs $12.00, so you are much better off getting premium seats.

Is a museum that contains some of the world’s foremost collections of photography and cinematographic technology. The museum holds photographs which are among the oldest and best in the world with photographs dating back from the introduction of photography in 1839 through to the present day. They also hold photographs made from all different processes, such as daguerreotype to digital. They have many rare photographs from many photographers from the 1800’s and up. This museum perfect to check out if you are into photography or are looking to educate yourself in the world of photography. Film, or Moving Image as the museum calls it, is another thing that the museum collects with about 28,000 titles in their collection spanning the entire history of international cinema so they surely have a vast amount to look from. They have preserved a collection of rare and unique 35mm prints on nitrate stock which was used in the beginning of the motion pictures through the middle of the twentieth century and is highly flammable. They also regularly screen film from their collection in the 500 seat Dryden Theatre, which is museum’s primary exhibition space for moving images.

If you are looking to get nostalgic with some of your favorite toys you had as a child then you could visit the National Toy Hall of Fame. Their collection of toys include toys, dolls, board games, video games, other electronic games, books, documents, and other historical materials related to play. They hold over 450,000 historical objects that are related to play. They have a huge collection of everything that has to do with Jim Henson and this includes Sesame Street and The Muppet Show. The museum also has a research library so that if you are looking to do a report on old toys or need some information on specific toys for whatever it is that you need help. The hours for the museum are Monday through Thursday from 10am to 5pm, Friday and Saturday from 10am to 8pm and Sunday from noon to 5pm. Admission to get in is $14 if you are 2 years or older and children under 2 get in for free. The hours for the research library are Monday through Friday from 10am to 5pm, but appointments are encouraged. You could also donate to the collection if there is favorite toy of yours that you never use anymore but is willing to have it taken in better care for an audience.
